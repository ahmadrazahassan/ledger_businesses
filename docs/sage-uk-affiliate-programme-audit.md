**Constraint (reviewer note):** Content production is handled separately by the publisher. This audit does **not** prescribe article titles or content deliverables; it states what the programme typically expects and what risks rejection.

## 1. Final Verdict

**Reject (for now)**

**Reasoning (direct):** Affiliate programmes at this tier approve **publishers who already demonstrate** editorial substance, UK relevance, and trustworthy monetisation — not only a clean template. Approval is usually withheld until there is **clear on-site evidence** of in-depth, original material aligned with Sage’s product set (reviews and comparisons that stand up to manual review), plus consistent trust and compliance signals. A minimal, professional shell **reduces** thin-affiliate risk but **does not substitute** for that evidence. Reapply when your **live content** and (where available) **organic engagement signals** match programme standards.

## 3. Evaluation Against Criteria

### 3.1 Brand & positioning

- **Editorial vs affiliate shell:** The site is positioned as editorial (independence, UK focus). **Risk:** If most pages are thin or link-heavy without methodology, reviewers will classify it as a **commercial doorway**.
- **UK focus:** Language, `en-GB`, HMRC/MTD/VAT/payroll context in copy (where present) supports UK positioning.
- **Homepage clarity:** Should state **who** (SMEs, sole traders, advisers), **what** (software evaluation and compliance context), and **how you make money** (disclosure path) — without feeling like a landing page for a single vendor.
- **Professional standards:** Minimal, non-gimmicky UI supports trust; avoid hype stats or unverifiable claims anywhere on the site.

### 3.2 Content relevance to Sage (assessment framework — no article list)

Reviewers typically look for **live URLs** that show:

- Depth and originality on **Sage Business Cloud Accounting** and **Sage Payroll** (not brochure rewrite).
- At least one **credible comparison** involving major UK alternatives (e.g. vs Xero or similar), written with **balanced criteria** and UK regulatory context.
- Coverage of **MTD, VAT, payroll, RTI** where relevant to product choice — integrated into analysis, not keyword stuffing.

**Thin or SEO-only pages** (short posts, duplicate roundups, pages that exist only to house links) are **primary rejection triggers**.

*Note:* Fulfilling this is your **content** workstream; the audit only flags that **absence of this evidence at application time** is the usual reason for “reject (for now).”

### 3.3 Compliance & trust signals

| Signal | Importance |
|--------|------------|
| Affiliate disclosure (standalone + near money actions) | **Critical** |
| Clear statement that reviews are **not paid for by vendors** (if true) | **High** |
| Privacy, terms, cookies | **Expected** |
| About with **identifiable editor/author** | **Expected** |
| Sage **trademark** use: correct naming; no imitation of Sage marketing assets; no copied Sage copy | **Critical** |

### 3.4 UX, design & technical quality

- **Clean / minimal / high-end:** Consistent spacing, typography, no cluttered ad stacks, no aggressive interstitials.
- **Navigation:** Logical hubs (e.g. categories covering reviews, payroll, tax) — naming should match user mental models (“reviews” / “guides” as appropriate).
- **Broken links:** Fix any dead internal anchors or footer links — looks negligent.
- **Performance & SSL:** Production must be **HTTPS**, fast LCP on key templates, no blocking third-party clutter.

### 3.5 Traffic quality (conceptual)

- Site should **look** built for organic UK search and referral — not traffic exchanges, incentivised clicks, or misleading ads.
- If asked, be ready to show **clean** acquisition (e.g. Search Console trends, sources). **Cannot be fixed by design alone.**

### 3.6 Fit for Sage brand

- **Tone:** Professional, supportive, precise — UK SME and adviser appropriate.
- **Sage positioning:** Honest fit (company size, complexity, payroll needs); avoid “best for everyone” hype.
- **Competitors:** Fair, criteria-based comparisons — **biased takedowns of competitors** backfire in partner review.

## 4. Critical Fixes Before Applying (10–15) — Non-Content Where Possible

1. **Homepage:** One clear value proposition line for **UK SMEs and advisers**; avoid generic superlatives (“leading authority”).
2. **Affiliate disclosure:** Explicit sentence: **editorial content is not paid for by software vendors** (only if accurate); how commissions work; reader cost unchanged.
3. **Money pages (when they exist):** Short disclosure block **before** primary affiliate CTAs, not only in the footer.
4. **About:** Add or tighten **editorial standards** (how products are evaluated, update policy, corrections).
5. **Sage branding:** Audit all pages for **correct product names**; never use Sage logos or assets in a way that implies official partnership **before** approval.
6. **Footer / nav:** No broken links; every “Explore” item must resolve to a real section or hub.
7. **Cookie / ads:** Ensure consent and ad scripts align with UK expectations (PECR/GDPR awareness in privacy copy).
8. **Contact:** Real channel for **editorial enquiries** — builds legitimacy.
9. **Remove unverifiable claims** (traffic figures, “#1” claims) sitewide.
10. **Design system:** No gradients or flashy marketing visuals if positioning as **editorial**; consistent components across templates.
11. **Mobile:** Touch targets, readable type, no horizontal scroll on nav rows.
12. **Metadata:** Titles/descriptions that reflect **independence** and **UK** — not keyword spam.
13. **Legal:** Terms and privacy dated and **en-GB** where dates appear.
14. **Programme application pack:** When you apply, prepare **URLs of your strongest live pages** — reviewers will click through; weak first impression fails fast.

**Content-side reality (one line):** Programme approval almost always requires **those strongest pages to exist and to be genuinely useful** — that work sits outside this document by your instruction.

## 5. Post-Approval Growth Roadmap (3–6 Months) — Strategic

*Assumes eventual approval and ongoing content investment (managed by you).*

| Phase | Focus |
|-------|--------|
| **Months 1–2** | Establish **3–5 flagship URLs** that reviewers would respect: deep product evaluation, comparison methodology, UK compliance hooks. Internal linking between Sage-relevant pages. |
| **Months 2–3** | **Conversion paths:** Clear CTAs to Sage products where editorially justified; track quality (not just volume). A/B test placement of disclosure vs friction. |
| **Months 3–4** | **Refresh** top pages after product/pricing changes; add FAQ/schema only where it improves clarity — not for spam. |
| **Months 4–6** | **Authority:** Accountant-relevant angles, payroll edge cases, MTD — always UK-grounded. Build repeat visits (newsletter, updates) without aggressive tactics. |
| **Ongoing** | **Partner relationship:** Responsive to Sage partner comms; no misleading promotions; annual disclosure review. |

**Conversions:** Long-term Sage partners optimise for **qualified** sign-ups (right customer profile), not maximum raw clicks — align content with **who Sage serves well**.
